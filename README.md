# Reported Questions — English Hub

A self-contained reference and practice page for the DSP English Hub. Covers reported questions in full: the core rules, tense shift table, structural comparison of wh- and yes/no types, and two interactive exercises with diagnostic feedback.

Designed for independent student use — homework, revision, or catch-up. Can also be used in class as a reference during activities.

---

## Content

### Reference (Sections 01–03)

**Section 01 — The Core Problem**
Four rule cards covering the fundamental changes when reporting questions:
1. Word order — no inversion, no auxiliary
2. Tense shift — one step back
3. Yes/no connector — *if* or *whether*
4. No question mark — reported questions are statements

**Section 02 — Tense Reference**
Full tense shift table: present simple → past simple, present continuous → past continuous, past simple → past perfect, present perfect → past perfect, will → would, can → could. With example sentences for each shift.

**Section 03 — Two Types**
Side-by-side comparison of wh-questions (use the wh-word) and yes/no questions (use *if* or *whether*), with three examples each. Note on *if* vs *whether*.

---

### Exercises (Section 04)

**Exercise A — Multiple Choice (4 questions)**
Students select the correctly reported form from three options. Each question targets a different error type:

| Q | Direct question | Error tested in distractors |
|---|----------------|-----------------------------|
| 1 | Where does your family live? | inversion (×2 variants) |
| 2 | Have you ever been to New York? | missing connector + tense; tense only |
| 3 | Why did you change your answer? | inversion; tense (present simple) |
| 4 | Will you come to the party? | will not shifted; inversion after whether |

Feedback is immediate on selection. Wrong answers name the specific error type. The correct option is highlighted after 1 second. Questions lock after first selection — no re-attempts.

**Exercise B — Transformation (5 questions)**
Students complete a reported sentence from a direct question. All five are typed and checked together via a Check button.

| Q | Direct question | Tense shift |
|---|----------------|-------------|
| 1 | What are you reading? | present continuous → past continuous |
| 2 | Did you finish the homework? | past simple → past perfect |
| 3 | How long have you been waiting? | present perfect continuous → past perfect continuous |
| 4 | Can you speak German? | can → could |
| 5 | Where will you be tomorrow? | will → would |

Wrong answers receive diagnostic feedback: tense error, inversion error, or generic with answer shown. Score displayed on check. Reset button clears all inputs and feedback.

## Usage

**Independent / homework**
Assign the URL. Students read, then complete both exercises. No login, no data collected, no submission — self-marking only.

**In class — reference during activity**
Keep open on a student device or projected. Useful during speaking tasks involving reported speech where students need to check a tense shift.

**In class — exercise walkthrough**
Exercise A works well whole-class on projector: show the question, take a vote, click, discuss the feedback. Exercise B can be set as silent individual work then compared in pairs.

## Known Limitations

- Exercise B answer matching is string-based. Unusual but valid phrasings (e.g. contracted forms, slightly different object pronouns) may occasionally be marked wrong.
- Exercise A does not reset — once all four MCQ items are answered the page must be reloaded to try again. Exercise B has a Reset button.
- The *if* vs *whether* note covers register difference but does not cover the subtle distinction in embedded infinitive constructions (*whether to go* vs *\*if to go*) — out of scope at this level.
